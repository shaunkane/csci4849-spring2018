      Purpose

      • Provide spatial and visual information to people with vision impairments
      • Maps, diagrams, some art
      • Can provide much needed access to independence, support learning (often in STEM fields)
      • Also useful for multimodal learners

                                              About Braille

                                              • 2 x 3 cells of a specified size (7.5mm tall)
                                              • Typically takes ~6 months to learn
                                              • Different grades Ð Grade 1 Braille is a letter-to-letter translation, Grade 2 involves contractions

                                                        Braille reading speed

                                                        • 124 wpm Ð Braille (Legge et al. 1999)
                                                        • 250-300 wpm Ð Adult reading speed (Bell 2001)
                                                        • 300-500 wpm Ð Screen reader (Borodin et al. 2010)
                                                        Slide 53

                                                        Things to know

                                                        • Braille must be kept to standard sizes and layouts or it can't easily be read
                                                        • Many blind and visually impaired people won't know alphabetic letter shapes
                                                        • Need orientation guides for Braille documents and tactile graphics

                                                          What do we know about tactile perception?

                                                          • Easier to perceive cutouts than raised items (Khalia et al. 2014)
                                                          • Provide space between textures to aid in disambiguation (BANA 2010)
                                                          • Tactile perception requires movement over an area (Hasty)
