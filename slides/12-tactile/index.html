                            Slide 28

                            Challenges in tactile graphics

                            • Experts needed to translate (traditionally often done by prisoners)
                            • Understanding effective tactile representations
                            • Handling superimposed visual information
                            • Labeling
                            • Supporting multimedia and moving information

                            We shouldn't expect teachers are great at this, or end users.

                            Slide 29

                            Tactile literacy

                            • Must be trained, not inherently known
                            • How to recognize different textures, identify shapes and markers
                            • Much of our understanding of images is inherently visual, not intuitive in non-visual contexts
                            • Whole-part learning

                            Easy to overestimate this.

                                                        About Braille

                                                        • 2 x 3 cells of a specified size (7.5mm tall)
                                                        • Typically takes ~6 months to learn
                                                        • Different grades – Grade 1 Braille is a letter-to-letter translation, Grade 2 involves contractions

                                                                  Braille reading speed

                                                                  • 124 wpm – Braille (Legge et al. 1999)
                                                                  • 250-300 wpm – Adult reading speed (Bell 2001)
                                                                  • 300-500 wpm – Screen reader (Borodin et al. 2010)
                                                                  Slide 61

                                                                  Things to know

                                                                  • Braille must be kept to standard sizes and layouts or it can't easily be read
                                                                  • Many blind and visually impaired people won't know alphabetic letter shapes
                                                                  • Need orientation guides for Braille documents and tactile graphics

                                                                    Slide 63

                                                                    What do we know about tactile perception?

                                                                    • Easier to perceive cutouts than raised items (Khalia et al. 2014)
                                                                    • Provide space between textures to aid in disambiguation (BANA 2010)
                                                                    • Tactile perception requires movement over an area (Hasty)
